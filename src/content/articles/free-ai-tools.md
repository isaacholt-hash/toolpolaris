## Free AI Tools by Category

### ✍️ Free AI Writing Tools

**Writesonic — Free plan: 10,000 words/month**
Writesonic's free tier covers blog posts, landing page copy, ad copy, and email sequences. The Article Writer 5.0 (available on paid plans) is better, but the free tier produces solid first drafts across most marketing formats. No credit card required.

→ Best for: Founders and freelancers testing AI writing before committing to a paid plan.

**Copy.ai — Free plan: 2,000 words/month + 90 days of Pro**
Copy.ai has one of the more generous free tiers in the writing category. The starter free plan gives you access to 90+ templates — ad copy, email subject lines, product descriptions, social captions — and 2,000 words per month indefinitely after the Pro trial ends.

→ Best for: Teams that need quick marketing copy across multiple formats.

**QuillBot — Free plan: unlimited paraphrasing (limited modes)**
QuillBot's free tier lets you paraphrase up to 125 words at a time with access to two rewriting modes. Primarily useful for refining AI-generated drafts rather than generating content from scratch.

→ Best for: Editors polishing AI or human first drafts.

### 📱 Free AI Social Media Tools

**Predis.ai — Free plan: 15 posts/month**
Predis.ai generates complete social media posts — image, caption, and hashtags — from a text prompt or URL. The free plan gives you 15 posts per month across Instagram, LinkedIn, Facebook, and Twitter. Genuinely useful for testing the tool's output before upgrading.

→ Best for: Solo marketers and small business owners who want AI to handle post creation end-to-end.

[Try Predis.ai free →](https://predis.ai?ref=isaac77) *(affiliate link)*

**Buffer — Free plan: 3 channels, 10 scheduled posts per channel**
Buffer's free tier is one of the most useful in social scheduling. You get 3 social channels (LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook, Twitter, TikTok, or Pinterest), up to 10 posts scheduled per channel at any time, and basic analytics. No AI content generation on the free tier, but solid scheduling infrastructure.

→ Best for: Solopreneurs and early-stage brands managing 1–3 social channels.

**Publer — Free plan: 3 social accounts, 10 posts queued**
Publer's free plan mirrors Buffer's scope but adds better visual post preview and a link-in-bio tool. Content recycling (one of Publer's strongest paid features) is not available on the free tier.

→ Best for: Creators who want to see exactly how posts will look before publishing.

[Try Publer free →](https://publer.com/toolpolaris) *(affiliate link)*

**Canva — Free plan: 250,000+ templates, basic AI tools**
Canva's free tier remains one of the most powerful in any category on this list. You get access to hundreds of thousands of templates, the Magic Write AI text tool (limited credits), and basic image editing. Magic Eraser and many premium AI features require Canva Pro ($15/mo). Still, for social graphics, presentations, and basic brand assets, the free tier is genuinely exceptional.

→ Best for: Anyone creating visual content for social media, email, or web.

### 🔎 Free AI SEO Tools

**Ubersuggest — Free plan: 3 searches/day**
Ubersuggest's free tier gives you keyword volume, difficulty scores, and top SERP pages for 3 queries per day. Useful for occasional keyword research; too limited for a high-volume content team.

→ Best for: Early-stage sites validating keyword opportunities before investing in a paid SEO tool.

**Google Search Console — Free, no limits**
Not technically an AI tool, but GSC's Performance reports and URL Inspection tool are essential free infrastructure for any SEO program. If you're spending on AI SEO tools without a verified GSC property, fix that first.

→ Best for: Every website, no exceptions.

**Ahrefs Webmaster Tools — Free for verified site owners**
Ahrefs' free webmaster tier gives you site audit data and backlink overview for any domain you own and verify. No keyword explorer on the free plan, but the technical audit and backlink data are genuinely useful.

→ Best for: Site owners who want Ahrefs-quality backlink data without the $99/mo subscription.

**Frase — Free plan: 1 document**
Frase offers a single free document on signup — enough to test their brief-generation workflow (which synthesises the top 20 SERP results into a structured brief) without committing. Not a sustainable free tier, but useful for evaluating the product.

→ Best for: Writers considering Frase who want to test the brief quality before paying.

### 🎨 Free AI Design Tools

**Adobe Firefly — Free plan: 25 generative credits/month**
Adobe Firefly is Adobe's AI image generator, trained on licensed content — which matters if you're using generated images commercially. The free tier gives 25 generative credits per month, enough to test the output quality and generate background images, stock photo alternatives, and social visuals.

→ Best for: Marketers who need legally safe AI-generated images for commercial use.

**Microsoft Designer — Free (with Microsoft account)**
Microsoft Designer uses DALL-E to generate images and social media graphics from prompts. It's free with any Microsoft account and integrates with Bing Image Creator. Output quality is competitive with Midjourney for simple social graphics.

→ Best for: Teams already using Microsoft 365 who want AI design without adding another subscription.

### 🤖 Free AI Automation Tools

**Zapier — Free plan: 100 tasks/month, 5 Zaps**
Zapier's free tier covers 5 automation workflows (Zaps) and 100 task executions per month. Enough to automate 1–2 recurring workflows: posting to social when a new blog post goes live, adding leads to a spreadsheet, or sending Slack notifications on form submissions.

→ Best for: Founders and small teams who want to test automation before committing to a paid plan.

**Make (formerly Integromat) — Free plan: 1,000 operations/month**
Make's free tier is more generous than Zapier's for complex multi-step workflows. You get 1,000 operations and can build workflows with multiple steps, filters, and conditions — useful for more sophisticated automations involving data transformation.

→ Best for: Technical marketers who need more complex workflow logic than Zapier's free tier allows.

### 💬 Free AI Chat / Research Tools

**ChatGPT — Free plan: GPT-4o (limited), GPT-3.5 (unlimited)**
The GPT-3.5 model is free without limits; GPT-4o is available on the free tier with usage caps. For marketing research, brief drafting, and ideation, free ChatGPT is sufficient for most tasks.

**Claude — Free plan: Claude Sonnet (limited daily usage)**
Anthropic's Claude free tier gives access to Claude Sonnet with daily usage limits. Claude's longer context window (compared to free ChatGPT) is useful for analysing long documents, auditing existing content, and drafting longer-form pieces.

**Perplexity — Free plan: 5 Pro searches/day + unlimited standard**
Perplexity's standard search (which cites sources) is free and unlimited — useful for quick research synthesis. Pro searches (GPT-4, Claude, larger result sets) are capped at 5/day on the free plan.

→ Best for: Marketers who want AI-powered research with citations rather than unchecked text generation.

## How to Build a Zero-Cost AI Marketing Stack

If you're starting from zero and want to test AI tools before spending, here's a functional free stack:

**Content creation:** ChatGPT (free) for ideation and first drafts → Canva (free) for visual assets

**Social media:** Predis.ai (15 free posts/month) + Buffer (3 channels free) for scheduling

**SEO:** Google Search Console (free) + Ahrefs Webmaster Tools (free for your site)

**Research:** Perplexity (free standard searches) for market and topic research

**Automation:** Zapier (5 free Zaps) to connect publishing → social → tracking

This stack handles the core workflow — create, publish, distribute, track — at zero monthly cost. The ceiling is volume: once you're creating more than 15 social posts per month or managing more than 3 channels, the free tiers become the constraint and it's worth upgrading.

## When to Upgrade from Free

The free tiers above are genuinely functional — but they have real limits. Here's when to upgrade:

- **Volume:** Publishing more than 3–4x per week across multiple channels will exhaust Buffer and Predis.ai free limits within weeks.
- **Quality ceiling:** Free writing tools (Copy.ai, Writesonic) produce noticeably weaker output than their paid tiers. If you're publishing content that needs to rank, the quality difference matters.
- **Team collaboration:** Most free tiers are single-user. Adding even one collaborator typically requires a paid plan.
- **Analytics:** Free tiers across every category strip out most performance data. If you're optimising campaigns, you need the data.

The right time to upgrade is when the free tier's limit is the constraint on your output — not before.
