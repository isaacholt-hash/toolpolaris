Content creation is the biggest time drain in most marketing teams. Writing, designing, filming, editing, scheduling — the average content marketer spends more time producing content than distributing or analysing it.

AI content creation tools have shifted this balance significantly. The teams using them well are producing 3–5x the content output with the same headcount. The teams that haven't adopted them yet are losing ground.

This guide covers the best AI tools across written, visual, and video content — and how to build a cohesive content creation stack.

## Video Content

### Descript — Best for Video Editing and Repurposing

Descript edits video by editing the transcript — delete a word from the text, and it's removed from the video. For marketers repurposing long-form video content into shorter clips, podcast editing, or social clips, it cuts editing time by 60–70%.

**AI features:** Overdub (AI voice that matches yours for re-recording corrections), Remove Filler Words (automatically removes "um," "uh," and false starts), and Studio Sound (one-click audio quality improvement).

**Pricing:** Free plan; Creator from $24/month.

---

### Opus Clip — Best for Short-Form Video Generation

Opus Clip takes long-form video (webinars, interviews, YouTube videos) and automatically identifies the most engaging moments, clips them, adds captions, and formats them for TikTok, Reels, and Shorts.

**Best for:** Repurposing existing video content into short-form social clips with minimal effort.

**Pricing:** Free plan; Pro from $19/month.

## The Most Common Mistake

The most common mistake when building an AI content creation stack is over-investing in writing tools and under-investing in distribution.

A stack that produces 50 pieces of content per month but distributes them poorly will generate less return than a stack that produces 20 pieces and gets each one in front of the right audience.

Distribution — email, SEO, paid, social — should be at least as important as production in your stack. Budget accordingly.
