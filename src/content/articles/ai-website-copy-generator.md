## What Makes Good Website Copy?

Before picking a tool, it's worth understanding what you're asking AI to produce. Good website copy:

- Leads with a clear, specific benefit (not a clever tagline)
- Addresses the visitor's primary objection within 3 seconds
- Uses hierarchy: headline → subheadline → social proof → CTA
- Speaks to one audience segment at a time
- Is scannable (short paragraphs, bold key phrases, visual breaks)

The tools that understand these principles — and bake them into their outputs — produce dramatically better results than generic AI writers.

## How to Get the Best Results from AI Website Copy

AI tools produce better website copy when you give them better inputs. Before generating any copy, have these ready:

**1. Your one-liner value prop**
"[Product] helps [target audience] do [specific outcome] without [common frustration]."

**2. Your top 3 features — as benefits**
Don't say "real-time collaboration." Say "your whole team sees changes instantly — no more version confusion."

**3. Your primary objection**
What's the #1 reason your target customer would *not* buy? Address it in the copy.

**4. Social proof type**
Do you have customer quotes? Case studies? Logos? User counts? Tell the AI what you have.

With these four inputs, even a $16/month tool like Writesonic will produce copy worth editing.

## What AI Website Copy Still Gets Wrong

AI copy generators in 2026 are genuinely impressive, but they still struggle with:

**Specificity:** Generic copy like "Boost your productivity" appears in thousands of websites. The more specific and unusual your value proposition, the harder AI finds it to articulate well.

**Emotional resonance:** AI can mimic the structure of emotional copy, but often misses the human nuance that makes copy feel real.

**Industry jargon balance:** Too much jargon sounds technical and cold. Too little sounds naive. AI frequently gets this wrong for specialised industries.

The solution: use AI to generate structure and first draft, then edit manually for specificity and voice. This hybrid approach is consistently faster and better than either AI alone or fully manual writing.
