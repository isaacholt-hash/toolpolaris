Not all AI copy generators are equal. Some produce generic filler that sounds like every other brand. The good ones — the ones that professional copywriters and performance marketers actually use — understand context, adapt to brand voice, and output copy that's genuinely usable without a full rewrite.

We ran the same briefs through five tools over three weeks. Here's what we found.

## What We Tested

For each tool, we ran identical prompts across four formats:
- **Facebook ad copy** (primary text + headline, hard sell)
- **Email subject lines** (A/B test pairs for promotional emails)
- **Landing page hero copy** (above-the-fold headline + subheadline)
- **Product descriptions** (200-word e-commerce descriptions)

Output was rated on persuasiveness, originality, brand-voice adaptability, and how much editing was required.

---

## 1. Copy.ai — Best Overall for Copywriting Workflows

**Best for:** Marketers and founders who write a lot of conversion-focused copy  
**Price:** Free plan (2,000 words/month); Pro from $36/month  
**Free plan:** Yes

Copy.ai is purpose-built for copywriting (not general writing), and it shows. Its **Campaign** feature lets you input a single product brief and generate a full suite of assets — email sequence, ad variations, landing page copy, and social posts — all maintaining consistent messaging. That's a genuinely different workflow from generating pieces one at a time.

The **Brand Voice** feature (Pro+) learns from your existing content and applies it consistently across outputs. After training on 3–4 sample pieces, the difference in output quality is significant.

**Best output in our tests:** Email subject lines and Facebook ad primary text.

## 2. Jasper — Best for High-Volume Teams

**Best for:** Content teams producing 50+ pieces of copy per month  
**Price:** From $39/month (Creator), $99/month (Pro, team features)  
**Free trial:** 7 days

Jasper has the largest template library of any tool here (50+ copy templates), the most advanced brand voice capabilities, and the best integrations — including a Chrome extension and a Jasper Everywhere browser integration that lets you use it inside Gmail, LinkedIn, and most other web apps.

For pure copy quality on ads and product descriptions, Jasper consistently produced the most polished first drafts in our tests — requiring the least editing before being usable. The downside is price: it's the most expensive option on this list.

[Check Jasper pricing →](https://jasper.ai?ref=toolpolaris) *(affiliate link)*

## 3. Writesonic — Best Value for Money

**Best for:** Freelancers and small businesses who need solid copy without agency pricing  
**Price:** Free plan (10,000 words/month); paid from $16/month  
**Free plan:** Yes (generous limits)

Writesonic's Chatsonic (its ChatGPT-style interface) and dedicated copy templates produce copy that's 85–90% as good as Jasper at roughly a third of the price. The **AI Article Writer** and **Sonic Editor** are genuinely good for combining SEO content and conversion copy in one workflow.

The free plan's 10,000 words/month (using GPT-3.5) is more than enough to evaluate whether the tool fits your workflow before upgrading.

[Check Writesonic pricing →](https://writesonic.com?ref=toolpolaris) *(affiliate link)*

## 4. Anyword — Best for Data-Driven Copy Optimisation

**Best for:** Performance marketers running paid ads who want predictive performance scores  
**Price:** From $39/month  
**Free trial:** 7 days

Anyword is the only tool in this list that scores copy against predicted conversion performance — not just quality. Each output comes with a **Predictive Performance Score** (0–100) based on its training data from high-performing ads. You can run multiple variations and instantly see which the algorithm predicts will perform best.

Whether those scores correlate with your specific audience is uncertain, but having a data signal for copy selection is genuinely useful when running A/B tests. Best for teams investing $10k+/month in paid media who want a more systematic approach to copy testing.

## The Verdict

For most marketers: **Copy.ai** (best workflow) or **Writesonic** (best value) are the right starting points. If you're running significant paid media budgets, add **Anyword** for its predictive scoring. If you need visuals too, **AdCreative.ai** replaces both a copywriter and a designer for ad creative specifically.

Don't over-invest in tools before you've established what copy formats you produce most. Match the tool to your actual workflow, not the most impressive demo.
