SEO tools have always been expensive. AI-powered SEO tools are now being sold at a premium on top of that. The question isn't whether AI improves SEO workflows — it clearly does — but which tools are worth the monthly subscription and which are charging AI prices for features that add marginal value.

This is an honest assessment. We're going to tell you which tools we'd actually pay for and which ones we wouldn't.

---

## What AI Actually Improves in SEO

Before reviewing tools, it's worth being specific about where AI adds genuine value in SEO workflows:

**Content briefs and optimisation:** AI can analyse the top 20 ranking pages for a keyword and tell you exactly what topics, terms, and structure your content needs. This used to take 2–3 hours of manual research. AI tools do it in 30 seconds.

**Keyword clustering:** Organising thousands of keywords into topic clusters manually is tedious. AI does it accurately and instantly.

**Content generation:** AI can produce a full SEO article draft in minutes. The draft needs editing, but it eliminates the blank page problem and reduces research time significantly.

**Technical SEO analysis:** Some AI tools now diagnose technical SEO issues and suggest fixes in plain language, without requiring you to interpret raw crawl data.

Where AI doesn't yet help much: link building strategy, local SEO nuance, and the kind of original research that earns links naturally. These still require human judgement.

### 2. Surfer SEO — Best for On-Page Optimisation

If Semrush is the research platform, Surfer is the optimisation tool. It does one thing — helps you write and optimise content that ranks — and it does it better than anything else.

**The Content Editor** is the product. Paste your draft, tell it your target keyword, and it gives you a real-time Content Score comparing your article against the top 20 ranking pages. The NLP analysis tells you exactly which terms are missing, which are over-represented, and what heading structure the algorithm favours.

**Best feature:** Surfer's SERP Analyser shows you exactly why the top-ranking pages rank — word count, keyword density, number of headings, page speed. Data-driven, not theoretical.

**Where it falls short:** Surfer isn't a keyword research tool. You still need Semrush, Ahrefs, or KeySearch for research. Surfer handles optimisation once you've chosen your targets.

**Pricing:** From $89/month. Affiliate commission: 25% recurring.

### 3. Frase — Best Value SEO + AI Writing Tool

Frase is the best-kept secret in SEO tools. At $14.99/month, it covers keyword research, content briefs, AI writing, and on-page optimisation — the core SEO workflow at a fraction of Semrush + Surfer's combined cost.

**What it does well:** Content brief creation is genuinely excellent. Input a keyword, and Frase analyses the top 20 ranking pages and builds a structured brief covering: recommended word count, headings to include, questions to answer, and topics to cover. For content teams producing SEO articles at scale, this cuts brief creation from 2–3 hours to under 10 minutes.

**Where it falls short:** Keyword research database is smaller than Semrush or Ahrefs. Competitive intelligence features are limited. Best used alongside a dedicated keyword research tool.

### 4. Ahrefs + AI Features — Best for Link Building & Backlink Research

Ahrefs remains the gold standard for backlink analysis and link building research. Its recent AI features — AI Content Grader, AI keyword clustering, and AI-generated content suggestions — add useful workflow tools without changing what Ahrefs is fundamentally strong at.

**Best AI feature:** The AI Content Grader compares your page against the top 10 ranking pages and flags specific gaps in topical coverage.

**Where it falls short:** Ahrefs' AI writing tools are less polished than Surfer or Frase. Use Ahrefs for research and link strategy; use Frase or Surfer for content optimisation.

**Pricing:** From $129/month.

## Tools That Weren't Worth It (In Our Testing)

Without naming specific products: several AI "SEO tools" in 2026 are essentially ChatGPT wrappers with SEO prompts, sold at $50–100/month. They produce generic content that won't rank. If a tool's core value proposition is "AI writes your SEO articles automatically," be sceptical — the output typically lacks the depth and originality that Google rewards.

The tools worth paying for are those with proprietary data (Semrush, Ahrefs, Surfer's NLP analysis, Frase's SERP data) — not those simply routing your prompts through a language model.
